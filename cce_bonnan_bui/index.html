Our story

Rooted in the music and culture of South Armagh

An Bonnán Buí CCÉ is the Comhaltas Ceoltóirí Éireann branch based in Crossmaglen, one of seven Comhaltas branches across County Armagh. We are part of an international cultural movement committed to the promotion and preservation of Irish music, song and dance.

Our branch takes its name from An Bonnán Buí, a celebrated poem in Irish by Cathal Buí Mac Giolla Ghunna, a lament for a yellow bittern that has been translated by some of the finest Irish poets including Seamus Heaney. The name connects us to the deep literary and musical traditions of our language and our land.

Over the years our members have competed and succeeded at the highest levels, with All Ireland medal winners at Fleadh Cheoil na hÉireann. We run regular events, sessions and concerts, including as part of the Lúnasa Festival and other community celebrations across South Armagh.

A place to learn, to play, to compete, and to keep the tradition alive for the next generation.

Céilí Bands and Fleadh Competition

As well as individual instrument classes, we enter céilí bands and groups into underage competitions at Fleadh Cheoil. This gives our younger members the chance to perform together, build confidence, and represent Crossmaglen at county and provincial level. Our next Fleadh is Fleadh Cheoil 2027 and preparations begin well in advance. If you want your child to be part of it, get in touch early.
